## Outreach Principles

### THE RULE OF ONE

Every outreach message has one ask. Not two. Not "check out our community AND book a call AND follow us." One clear next step.

### LEAD WITH THEIR WORLD

The first sentence is about them — not Aurigen, not Lando, not tax liens. Research them first. Reference something specific. Show you actually know who they are.

### EARN THE ASK

Give something before you ask for anything. A useful data point, a genuine compliment that's specific, a resource they can actually use. Never lead with the pitch.

### SHORT WINS

First messages are never longer than 150 words. If they respond, then you can say more. The goal of the first message is one response — not a sale.

## Message Formats

### PODCAST PITCH EMAIL

Subject: [Specific episode reference] — tax lien angle your audience hasn't heard
Structure:

- Line 1: Specific reference to their show (episode, topic, or guest)
- Line 2: One sentence on who Lando is — specific, credible, not generic
- Line 3: The angle — what Lando would talk about that their audience hasn't heard
- Line 4: One data point that proves the topic is worth 45 minutes
- Line 5: The ask — one sentence, one option
- Total: Under 150 words

### COMMUNITY PARTNERSHIP DM

Structure:

- Line 1: Specific compliment on their community — not generic
- Line 2: Who you are in one sentence
- Line 3: The value exchange — what Aurigen offers their members
- Line 4: The ask — a 15-minute call or a guest post, not a full partnership pitch
- Total: Under 100 words

### INFLUENCER COLLABORATION DM

Structure:

- Line 1: Reference a specific piece of their content — be real about it
- Line 2: One sentence on Aurigen and why it's relevant to their audience
- Line 3: The collaboration idea — specific, simple, low-lift for them
- Line 4: The ask — yes or no question
- Total: Under 80 words

### COLD INVESTOR EMAIL

Subject: [Their state] has a [X]% tax lien rate right now
Structure:

- Line 1: The data point — specific to their state or region (pull from states-en.js)
- Line 2: One sentence on what Aurigen is
- Line 3: The free offer — join the community, no pitch
- Line 4: The link
- Total: Under 100 words

### FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE (when no response)

**Follow-up 1 — Day 5:** One sentence. Bump the original. No apology for following up.
**Follow-up 2 — Day 12:** New angle. Different data point. Same ask.
**Follow-up 3 — Day 20:** The graceful exit. Give them an easy out. Leave the door open.
Never send more than 3 follow-ups.

## Research Protocol

Before writing any outreach message:

1. Look them up — know their content, their audience, their recent work
2. Find one specific thing to reference — not "I love your content"
3. Pull relevant state data from states-en.js for their geography
4. Identify the one thing Aurigen offers that their audience actually needs

## Rules You Never Break

- Never reference any specific seminar brand or instructor by name
- Never open with "I hope this email finds you well"
- Never open with "My name is" — they'll read your name in the signature
- Never pitch the full product in the first message
- Never send a follow-up the day after — minimum 5 days
- Never make income claims without standard disclaimer
- Always research before writing — no generic messages
