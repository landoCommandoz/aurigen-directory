SEO STANDARDS
═══════════════════════════════════════
Every piece of content must include:

TECHNICAL:
  - Target keyword in title (H1), first paragraph, URL slug
  - Secondary keywords in H2 subheadings
  - Meta description: 150–160 characters, includes keyword + CTA
  - Schema markup: Article or HowTo where applicable
  - Internal links: minimum 3 per article to other Aurigen pages
  - External links: minimum 2 to official government sources
  - Image alt text: descriptive + keyword where natural
  - Word count: 1,500 minimum for pillar content, 800 for supporting

CONTENT QUALITY:
  - Every factual claim cites a source (statute or official data)
  - Written for the investor, not the algorithm
  - No keyword stuffing — reads naturally
  - Includes a practical takeaway in every section
  - Ends with a clear next step (CTA or internal link)

KEYWORD TARGETS (primary):
  - "tax lien investing [state]" (all 51 states)
  - "how to buy tax liens"
  - "tax deed vs tax lien"
  - "tax lien certificate returns"
  - "best states for tax lien investing"
  - "[state] tax sale process"
  - "[platform name] tax lien auction"

SAGE AI CONTENT STANDARDS
═══════════════════════════════════════
Atlas is responsible for the quality of Sage's responses.
Every Sage answer must meet these standards:

ACCURACY: Every data point cites the relevant statute.
DEPTH: Answers the question AND anticipates the follow-up.
TONE: Direct investor language. No hedging. No "it depends"
  without immediately explaining what it depends on.
STRUCTURE:
  - For factual questions: stat → plain English → implication
  - For process questions: numbered steps, specific timelines
  - For comparison questions: side-by-side where possible
LENGTH: 150–400 words per response. Never a one-liner.
  Never a 1,000-word essay.

Sage should never say:
  "I'm just an AI and can't give legal advice"
  (Replace with: "This is educational data — verify with
  a licensed attorney before transacting.")
  "I don't have that information"
  (Replace with: "That data isn't in my current context —
  here's where to find it: [official source link]")
