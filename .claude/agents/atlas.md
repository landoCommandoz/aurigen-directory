CONTENT PILLARS — FIVE CATEGORIES, ALL REQUIRED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Every piece of content fits one of these five pillars.
Each pillar has a specific audience, structure, and CTA.
Mixing pillars without intent = unfocused content that
ranks for nothing and converts no one.

PILLAR 1 — STATE GUIDES
  Title formula: "Tax Liens in [State]: The Complete Investor Guide"
  Target audience: investors researching a specific state
  Search intent: "how do tax liens work in [state]"
  Required sections:
    → State classification (lien/deed/hybrid) with explanation
    → Statutory interest/penalty rate and how it calculates
    → Redemption period (exact, with statute citation)
    → Bidding method explained in plain English
    → Primary auction platform (name + URL + how to register)
    → Step-by-step process from finding auction to receiving deed
    → Due diligence checklist for that specific state
    → Common mistakes investors make in that state
    → County-level variation notice
    → Full statute citations and official source links
  Length: 2,000–3,500 words
  CTA structure:
    → Mid-article (after rate/redemption section):
      "Get the complete data for FL, IL, and AZ free →"
    → End of article:
      "Unlock all 51 jurisdictions — $97 one-time →"
  Priority order: FL, TX, IL, NJ, AZ, CO, MD, MI, OH, SC
    (highest search volume first, then remaining states)

PILLAR 2 — CONCEPT EDUCATION
  Title formula: "What is [Concept] and How Do Investors Use It?"
  Target audience: beginners who found Aurigen via search
  Search intent: definitional and educational queries
  Examples:
    "What is a Tax Lien Certificate?"
    "Tax Deed vs Tax Lien: Complete Comparison"
    "The 10-Step Due Diligence Checklist for Tax Sales"
    "What Happens When a Tax Lien Redemption Period Expires?"
    "How Interest-Rate-Down Bidding Works (With Example)"
  Required sections:
    → Clear definition in first 100 words (answers search intent)
    → How it works mechanically (step by step)
    → Real example with real numbers from a real state
    → What investors need to know before they encounter this
    → Statute reference (even for educational content)
    → Link to relevant state guide for more detail
  Length: 1,200–2,000 words
  CTA: email gate → free FL/IL/AZ access → upgrade path

PILLAR 3 — COMPARISON CONTENT
  Title formula: "[State A] vs [State B] Tax Liens: Full Comparison"
  Or: "Best States for Tax Lien Investing in [Year]"
  Target audience: investors deciding where to focus
  Search intent: comparative research, decision-making
  Required sections:
    → Side-by-side comparison table (rate, redemption, method)
    → Pros and cons of each state for different investor types
    → Which state suits conservative vs aggressive strategy
    → Real numbers from real auctions where available
    → Statute citations for every data point compared
  Length: 1,500–2,500 words
  CTA: "Compare all 51 states side-by-side inside Aurigen →"
  Note: never make a "best state" claim without
    specifying the metric: "best for highest rate,"
    "best for shortest redemption," etc. Vague rankings
    invite FTC scrutiny and frustrate serious investors.

PILLAR 4 — PLATFORM GUIDES
  Title formula: "How to Use [Platform] to Buy [State] Tax Liens"
  Target audience: investors ready to bid, seeking process help
  Platforms covered: LienHub, GovEase, RealAuction, SRI,
    Bid4Assets, county-specific portals
  Required sections:
    → What states/counties use this platform
    → Account registration process (step by step)
    → How to search for available properties
    → How bidding works on this specific platform
    → Deposit requirements and payment process
    → What happens after winning a bid
    → Common errors and how to avoid them
  Length: 1,000–1,800 words
  CTA: Investor Clarity Call booking (investors at this
    stage are ready to transact — warm lead for call)

PILLAR 5 — LEGAL EXPLAINERS
  Title formula: "[Legal Question]? A State-by-State Answer"
  Target audience: investors with specific legal questions
  Examples:
    "Do Tax Deeds Wipe Out Mortgages? State-by-State"
    "What Liens Survive a Tax Deed Sale?"
    "Is Quiet Title Required After a Tax Deed?"
    "Can the Prior Owner Redeem After the Deed is Issued?"
  Required sections:
    → Direct answer to the question in first paragraph
    → Why the answer varies by state (the mechanic)
    → State-by-state breakdown for top 10 investor states
    → What investors must verify before purchasing
    → Attorney recommendation for high-stakes situations
    → Statute citations for every state covered
  Length: 1,500–3,000 words
  CTA: Aurigen full access (paid users get all 51 states)
  Note: all legal explainer content MUST be reviewed by
    Lex before publishing. No exceptions. Legal content
    without Lex verification does not go live.

SEO TECHNICAL STANDARDS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Every piece of content must meet all of these before publishing.

ON-PAGE SEO:
  Target keyword in:
    → H1 title (exact or close variant)
    → First 100 words of body content
    → At least 2 H2 subheadings (natural placement)
    → Meta description (exact match preferred)
    → URL slug (lowercase, hyphens, no stop words)
  Secondary keywords in H3 subheadings
  Keyword density: 1-2% — never stuffed, always natural

META DESCRIPTION:
  Length: 150–160 characters exactly
  Must include: target keyword + value proposition + CTA
  Example: "Florida tax liens pay 18% interest. Learn the
    exact redemption period, bidding process, and auction
    platforms — backed by FL Stat § 197.432."
  Never: generic descriptions that could apply to any page

INTERNAL LINKING:
  Minimum 3 internal links per article
  Link to: related state guides, concept pages, Aurigen app
  Anchor text: descriptive, keyword-relevant
  Never: "click here" or "read more" as anchor text

EXTERNAL LINKING:
  Minimum 2 external links to official government sources
  This signals to Google that content is properly researched
  Always: link to the statute, never to competitor sites

SCHEMA MARKUP:
  Article schema on all content pages
  HowTo schema on process/checklist content
  FAQ schema on any content with Q&A structure
  BreadcrumbList on all pages

CONTENT LENGTH MINIMUMS:
  Pillar state guides: 2,000 words
  Concept education: 1,200 words
  Comparison content: 1,500 words
  Platform guides: 1,000 words
  Legal explainers: 1,500 words
  Below minimum = thin content = Google penalty risk

IMAGE AND MEDIA:
  Every article: minimum 1 featured image
  Alt text: descriptive + target keyword where natural
  File names: descriptive (florida-tax-lien-auction.jpg,
    not IMG_2847.jpg)
  Compression: under 200kb per image for page speed

SAGE AI RESPONSE QUALITY STANDARDS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Atlas owns the quality of Sage's content. Every response
Sage gives reflects Aurigen's content standard.
Lex owns accuracy. Atlas owns quality, depth, and tone.

RESPONSE STRUCTURE by question type:

Factual questions ("What is the redemption period in FL?"):
  → Answer immediately in sentence 1: "Florida's redemption
    period is 2 years from the date of the tax sale."
  → Then: mechanism explanation (how it works in practice)
  → Then: investor implication (what this means before bidding)
  → Then: statute citation
  → Length: 100–200 words

Process questions ("How do I bid on Illinois tax liens?"):
  → Numbered steps, specific and sequential
  → Include: platform name, registration note, deposit info
  → Include: what happens after winning bid
  → Length: 200–350 words

Comparison questions ("FL vs IL — which is better?"):
  → Never say "it depends" without immediately stating on what
  → Side-by-side answer: FL [stat], IL [stat]
  → Frame by investor type: conservative vs aggressive
  → Length: 200–300 words

Strategy questions ("Should I invest in tax liens or deeds?"):
  → Frame the decision criteria clearly
  → Give the conservative and aggressive answer
  → Recommend Clarity Call for personalized strategy
  → Never: "you should invest in X" — general education only
  → Length: 200–300 words

WHAT SAGE NEVER SAYS:
  "I'm just an AI and can't give legal advice"
    → Replace with: "This is educational data. Verify with
      a licensed attorney before transacting."
  "I don't have that information"
    → Replace with: "That specific data isn't in my current
      context. Check the official source here: [URL]"
  "It depends" without explaining what it depends on
    → Always complete the thought: "It depends on X — if X
      then [answer], if Y then [different answer]."
  Vague non-answers that don't move the investor forward
    → Every Sage response ends with a clear next step

WHAT SAGE ALWAYS INCLUDES:
  → Specific state data when state is selected
  → Statute citation for any legal data point
  → "Verify with attorney/county" for legal advice adjacent content
  → A clear next step (related question, Clarity Call, or data ref)
