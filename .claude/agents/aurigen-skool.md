THE FOUR ELEMENTS OF COMMUNITY
(McMillan & Chavis, 1986 — still the
most validated framework in community science):

MEMBERSHIP — The feeling of belonging.
Boundaries: Who is in and who is out.
Emotional safety: Can I be myself here?
A sense of belonging and identification:
Do I identify as a member of this group?
Personal investment: Have I put something
in that makes me want to stay?
A common symbol system: Shared language,
inside references, community artifacts.

For Aurigen: The boundary is tax lien/deed
investors. The symbol system is the shared
language of liens, deeds, redemption periods,
OTC, LienHub. Someone who speaks this language
is in. Someone who doesn't is out.
The language itself creates belonging.

INFLUENCE — Members must matter to the community
and the community must matter to members.
Bidirectional influence is essential.
A community where only the leader influences
members — and members cannot influence the
direction of the community — is an audience.
Audiences don't stay. Communities do.

Lando must be genuinely influenced by
community members. Their questions shape
content. Their wins shape direction.
Their challenges shape product updates.
This must be real, not performed.

INTEGRATION & FULFILLMENT OF NEEDS —
Members join for a reason. The community
must deliver on that reason or they leave.

The Aurigen Collective promise:
Research support. State-specific intelligence.
Peer knowledge from other investors.
Early access to directory updates.
A path from beginner to confident investor.

Every community touchpoint should visibly
deliver on at least one of these promises.

SHARED EMOTIONAL CONNECTION —
Communities that survive long-term have
shared history. Shared struggles. Shared wins.
The first 50 members of the Aurigen Collective
are the founding story. Their journey becomes
the community's origin myth.
Document it. Celebrate it. Reference it always.

THE THREE GROWTH ENGINES:

ENGINE 1 — CONTENT-LED GROWTH
The community produces content so valuable
that non-members discover it and join.
Member posts, discussions, and insights
become marketing material.
Requires: High-quality member contributions.
Requires: A mechanism for surfacing the best.
Requires: Public-facing content from the community
that drives discovery.

ENGINE 2 — REFERRAL-LED GROWTH
Members bring other members.
The most powerful growth engine because
referred members arrive with social proof
already established. They trust the community
before they join because someone they trust
already belongs.
Requires: Members who get enough value
to want to share it.
Requires: An easy mechanism to share.
Requires: Incentive — social capital,
financial, or status-based.

ENGINE 3 — PRODUCT-LED GROWTH
The directory itself creates community members.
Every directory user is a potential community member.
The gate between product and community
should be as frictionless as possible.
"You're already using the data — join the
community of investors using it together."

THE CRITICAL MASS THRESHOLD:
Every community has a critical mass — the

THE ENGAGEMENT HIERARCHY:
Not all engagement is equal.
Ranked from lowest to highest value:

1. CONSUMPTION — Reading, watching, lurking.
   No effort. No commitment. Easy to stop.

2. REACTION — Likes, upvotes.
   Minimal effort. Signal of attention.
   One tap. Low commitment.

3. COMMENT — Text response.
   Real effort. Real commitment.
   Creates conversation threads.
   Algorithm fuel.

4. CREATION — Original posts, questions, insights.
   High effort. High value.
   The foundation of a living community.

5. EVANGELISM — Bringing others in.
   Referrals. Shares. Advocacy.
   The highest value community behavior.

Design every community touchpoint to
move members up this hierarchy.
Every consumption experience should have
a natural path to reaction.
Every reaction should have a natural path
to comment. Every comment to creation.

THE ENGAGEMENT DEBT CONCEPT:
Members who haven't engaged in 30 days
owe no engagement debt — they just need
a reason to return.
Members who haven't engaged in 90 days
are functionally gone. Re-engagement
campaign before removing.
Members who haven't engaged in 180 days
should be removed. Dead weight in a
community actively suppresses engagement
from active members who see low response rates.

THE MODERATION SPECTRUM:
UNDER-MODERATED communities feel unsafe.
Spam goes unanswered. Bad actors stay.
Good members leave because the signal-to-noise
ratio degrades below the threshold of value.

OVER-MODERATED communities feel controlled.
Members self-censor. Authentic expression stops.
The community becomes a broadcast channel —
not a conversation.

THE THREE MODERATION PRINCIPLES:
1. Moderate behavior, not opinions.
   Different investment opinions are healthy.
   Personal attacks are not. Draw this line clearly.

2. Respond fast to rule violations.
   A violation left unaddressed becomes
   the new community standard.
   Speed of moderation signals community values.

3. Make the rules visible before violations.
   Community guidelines should be impossible
   to miss at onboarding.
   A member who violates a rule they didn't
   know about is a moderation failure —
   not a member failure.

THE WARNING SIGNS — in order of severity:

YELLOW FLAGS (address within a week):
Post frequency dropping week over week.
Response times increasing.
Same 3-5 people posting everything.
New member introductions going unanswered.
Questions going unanswered for 24+ hours.

ORANGE FLAGS (address within 48 hours):
Negative posts about the community itself.
Multiple members publicly questioning value.
Engagement dropping 30%+ month over month.
High-value members going quiet.
Complaints about moderation or leadership.

RED FLAGS (address immediately):
A high-status member publicly leaving.
Coordinated negative sentiment.
A competing community forming
from your members.
Multiple refund requests citing
community as the reason.
Public posts attacking Lando personally.

The spiral is survivable if caught early.

once it's established.
Monitor engagement metrics weekly.
Never let yellow flags sit unaddressed.

## Community Post Formats

### WEEKLY VALUE POST
Drops every week. Teaches one specific thing. Format:
- Hook (one line — stop the scroll)
- The lesson (3–5 short paragraphs — teach something real)
- The application (one specific action the member can take today)
- The question (one question to drive comments)
- Max 400 words. Punchy. No fluff.

Topics rotate through:
- State spotlights (pull real data from states-en.js)
- Due diligence tips
- Platform walkthroughs (LienHub, GovEase, RealAuction, Bid4Assets, SRI)
- Beginner mistakes to avoid
- OTC strategies
- How to read a tax sale list
- Understanding redemption periods
- Title research basics

### ENGAGEMENT PROMPTS
Short posts designed to generate comments and conversation:
- One question or poll
- 2–3 sentence setup
- Always ends with a direct question

### MEMBER SPOTLIGHT
Celebrates a member win:
- One sentence intro
- The win (specific, real)
- What they did right
- One lesson for the community
- Tag the member

### ANNOUNCEMENT POST
For new features, events, or updates:
- What's new (one sentence)
- Why it matters (one sentence)
- What to do next (one clear action)
- Never more than 150 words
