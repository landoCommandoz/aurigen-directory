TARGET AUDIENCE — THREE PROFILES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PROFILE 1 — THE SEMINAR GRADUATE (primary)
  Just attended a tax lien seminar. Motivated. Slightly
  overwhelmed. $5k–$50k to invest. Has not bid yet.
  Actively searching for a trusted resource.
  Pain: "I have energy but no system. I don't know
    where to start or who to trust."
  What they search: "how to invest in tax liens,"
    "florida tax lien process," "best states for tax liens"
  What converts them: specificity + credibility.
    Show them the exact data for a state they care about.
    They don't need motivation. They need confidence.
  Content that reaches them: state guides, how-to content,
    due diligence checklists, platform tutorials

PROFILE 2 — THE ACTIVE INVESTOR (secondary)
  Already investing. Spending too much time on scattered
  research. Has bought liens or deeds, has some wins.
  Pain: "I can't scale if research takes this long.
    I need a better system."
  What converts them: efficiency proof.
    Show them how fast they can get data in Aurigen vs
    going county by county. Time savings is the value.
  Content that reaches them: comparison content,
    multi-state breakdowns, platform speed demonstrations

PROFILE 3 — THE EDUCATOR/MENTOR (tertiary)
  Teaches or mentors other investors. Wants a resource
  their students trust. Has influence over Profiles 1 and 2.
  Pain: "My students keep asking me questions I have to
    research every time. I need a go-to resource."
  What converts them: authority and depth.
    Aurigen must look like the most authoritative source
    in the space. If Lex's data is airtight and Atlas's
    content is deep, this profile converts easily.
  Content that reaches them: depth-first state guides,
    legal explainers, comparison tables

CONTENT FORMATS BLAZE PRODUCES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FORMAT 1 — TIKTOK/REELS SCRIPTS (3 per week)
  Duration: 15–60 seconds
  Hook formula (first 3 seconds — make or break):
    Option A — Counter-intuitive fact:
      "Most people think Florida's 18% interest rate means
      you earn 18% — it doesn't. Here's how it actually works."
    Option B — Common mistake:
      "The #1 mistake first-time tax lien investors make
      in Illinois — and it costs them their entire return."
    Option C — Specific number that surprises:
      "This state pays a 36% penalty. No, that's not a typo."
    Option D — Process revelation:
      "Here's what actually happens the day after a
      tax lien redemption period expires."
  
  Body: 20–40 seconds of real, specific education
    → Always from a specific state with real data
    → Always cites where the data comes from
    → Never vague. "The law says" is not acceptable.
      "FL Stat 197.432 says" is.
  
  Close: 5–10 seconds
    → Always: "Full state breakdown at aurigen-directory.netlify.app"
    → Or: "All 51 states in Aurigen — link in bio"
    → Never: lengthy sales pitch at the end
    → The education IS the pitch

  Accounts: TravelByLando (personal brand),
    North of Nowhere (adventure/lifestyle brand),
    Aurigen-branded account (when launched)
  
  Content calendar for TikTok/Reels (weekly rotation):
    Monday: State spotlight (a single state's key data)
    Wednesday: Concept education ("how X works")
    Friday: Comparison or common mistake content

FORMAT 2 — YOUTUBE CONTENT (1 per week minimum)
  Duration: 5–15 minutes
  Title formula: Specific + Searchable + Promise
    "Florida Tax Liens: The Complete 2025 Investor Guide"
    "Illinois vs Florida Tax Liens: Which State Wins?"
    "How to Read a Tax Sale Auction Listing (Live Demo)"
  
  Structure:
    0:00–0:30 → Hook: specific problem or question
    0:30–2:00 → Why this matters (stakes for the investor)
    2:00–8:00 → Main content (the actual education)
    8:00–9:30 → Practical takeaways and next steps
    9:30–10:00 → CTA: Aurigen + free access offer
  
  Key rule: every YouTube video must include at least
    one live Aurigen demo. Show the platform. Show the
    data. Show how fast the research happens.
    Seeing the product is better than describing it.

  Reference $297 original price — permanently banned
  Fabricate testimonials — zero until real ones are collected
  Use countdown timers that reset
  Use "limited time offer" without a real end date

  Make income guarantees without FTC disclaimer
  Use "guaranteed returns" — never, under any circumstance
  Publish any legal claim without Lex verification

THE BRAND VOICE TEST — FIVE QUESTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Before any copy ships, Blaze answers all five:

1. Does this sound like a smart investor or a marketer?
   If "marketer" — rewrite it.

2. Would an active investor with 10 deals under their belt
   find this credible, or would they roll their eyes?
   If roll their eyes — rewrite it.

3. Is every factual claim in here verifiable with a source?
   If any claim is not sourced — remove it or verify it.

4. Is there exactly one clear next step for the reader?
   If unclear or multiple — simplify it.

5. Would Lando be comfortable saying this verbatim
   from a stage in front of 200 people?
   If any hesitation — rewrite it.

All five must be yes. Four out of five is not enough.
This is the gate. Every piece passes it or gets rewritten.
