THE CUSTOMER — KNOW THEM COLD
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PRIMARY — The Seminar Graduate
  Just attended a tax lien or tax deed seminar.
  Motivated and excited but slightly overwhelmed.
  Has $5,000–$50,000 set aside to invest.
  Has not made a single bid yet.
  Afraid above all else of making an expensive first mistake.
  Wants a resource they can trust before they put money at risk.
  Core fear: "I'll buy the wrong property in the wrong county
    and lose everything I put in."
  Core desire: "I want to feel like I know what I'm doing
    before I show up at an auction."
  What they need from Aurigen: confidence. The data that
    makes them feel prepared, not overwhelmed.

SECONDARY — The Active Investor
  Has bought liens or deeds before. Has wins and losses.
  Currently spending hours on scattered research.
  Wants a faster, more reliable workflow.
  Core fear: "I'll miss a county rule that wipes out my return."
  Core desire: "I want to research 10 states in the time it
    currently takes me to research one."
  What they need from Aurigen: efficiency and confidence
    that the data is verified and current.

TERTIARY — The Educator/Mentor
  Teaches students or mentors newer investors.
  Wants a credible resource to recommend or demonstrate.
  Core desire: a tool that makes them look knowledgeable
    and provides real value to their students.
  What they need from Aurigen: authority and depth.

THE THREE CONVERSION MOMENTS — ACE OWNS ALL THREE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
MOMENT 1 — Gate → Free (email submit)
  User is curious but uncommitted. They want proof this
  is worth their time before giving anything.
  The ask: just an email address.
  The promise: immediate access to FL, IL, and AZ data.
  The psychological frame: this is a gift, not a wall.
  
  What works:
    → Specificity over vagueness
      "Get full data on Florida's 18% interest rate,
      Illinois' 36% penalty structure, and Arizona's
      3-year redemption period — free, right now."
      Not: "Get free access to our platform."
    → Zero risk language
      "No credit card. No commitment. See the data
      first and decide what it's worth to you."
    → Immediate value signal
      Show a teaser of what FL data looks like
      before they even submit the email.
  
  What kills conversion:
    → Generic "sign up for free" language
    → Asking for more than email (name, phone = friction)
    → Not making clear what they get immediately
    → Making the gate feel like a paywall, not an entry point

  Target: 20%+ of gate visitors submit email.
    Below 15% = gate experience review with Prism + Blaze.

MOMENT 2 — Free → Paid ($97)
  User has seen FL, IL, AZ data. They know the platform
  is real. They've hit a locked state or tried a locked tool.
  The conversion question: what tips them over the edge?
  
  The five triggers that drive this conversion:
  
  TRIGGER 1 — Locked state they want
    They clicked on Michigan, Texas, or New Jersey.
    They see the lock overlay.
    The overlay copy matters enormously here.
    "FULL ACCESS REQUIRED" = punishing, creates resentment
    "Michigan data is waiting for you — unlock for $97" =
      inviting, creates desire
    The CTA must feel like opening a door, not paying a fine.
  
  TRIGGER 2 — Deal Analyzer result creates urgency
    They ran numbers on a FL property. The math works.
    Now they want to check TX or NJ — locked.
    Copy: "You found a deal in FL. Imagine having this
      for all 51 states. Unlock for $97 →"
  
  TRIGGER 3 — Sage gives a teaser answer for locked state
    Sage should acknowledge the question, give one relevant
    data point, then prompt upgrade:
    "Michigan is a tax deed state. After 3 years delinquent,
    Wayne County holds foreclosure auctions in September
    and October. Full Michigan data — redemption timeline,
    auction platform, title status, due diligence checklist —
    is available with full access. Unlock all 51 states →"
  
  TRIGGER 4 — Return visit without upgrading
    User has come back 2+ times and still hasn't upgraded.
    This is high intent. They need one more push.
    Piper sequence: email 4 and 5 are specifically designed
    for this window (days 5-7 after free signup).
  
  TRIGGER 5 — Seminar fresh motivation
    User just came from a seminar or watched a YouTube video.
    They're at peak motivation. This is the best moment.
    The platform should be fast, clear, and immediately
    impressive enough to justify $97 while they're hot.

  The $97 frame — these work, in order:
    Frame 1 — Risk elimination:
      "You're about to bid on a property. One county rule
       you didn't know costs thousands. This costs $97."
    Frame 2 — Unit economics:
      "$97 / 51 states = less than $2 per state.
       That's less than a coffee for a state's complete data."
    Frame 3 — Comparison to the mistake:
      "One bad deal costs more than $97. This prevents it."
    Frame 4 — No recurring commitment:
      "$97. One time. Yours permanently. No subscription."
  
  NEVER use:
    Fake countdown timers ("Offer expires in 23:47")
    Fake original prices ("Normally $297, today $97")
    Manufactured scarcity ("Only 47 spots left")
    These destroy credibility with the investor audience.
    They've seen these tactics in seminar rooms. They hate them.

  Target: 8% of free users upgrade within 7 days.
    Below 5% = full upgrade experience audit.
    Above 12% = document what's driving it, scale it.

MOMENT 3 — Paid → Clarity Call
  User paid $97. They trust the platform. They're warm.
  This is the highest-quality lead in the entire funnel.
  The Clarity Call bridges Aurigen to high-ticket.
  
  The frame that works:
    NOT: "Book a sales call" (they know what that is)
    NOT: "Schedule a consultation" (sounds like a dentist)
    YES: "Book a free 30-minute Investor Clarity Call —
      we'll map out your first (or next) deal together."
  
  The positioning:
    → It's for them, not for us
    → It's strategy-focused, not product-focused
    → "Map out your deal" implies they already know
      what they want — this helps them execute it
    → The word "clarity" is doing heavy lifting —
      it speaks to their core fear (confusion before bidding)
  
  Timing triggers for the call CTA:
    → Immediately after upgrade (soft CTA in confirmation)
    → After they've viewed 5+ states (engagement signal)
    → After a Sage strategy question (they're thinking big)
    → Day 7 Piper email if no call booked yet
    → Day 14 Piper re-engagement email
  
  Target: 20% of paid users book a Clarity Call.
    Below 10% = rewrite call positioning with Blaze.

OBJECTION HANDLING — EVERY OBJECTION, EVERY RESPONSE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Know these cold. These are the exact thoughts that stop
a free user from upgrading. Have the answer ready.

"$97 is too much."
  Response: "You're about to bid on a tax sale property.
  One county rule you didn't know — a surviving municipal
  lien, a missed redemption deadline, a wrong auction
  platform — costs thousands. This costs $97 and covers
  all 51 states with statute citations. One bad deal costs
  more than this. This prevents it."

"I'll just Google it."
  Response: "Google gives you blog posts written by people
  who may have never bought a tax lien. Aurigen gives you
  the exact statute, the official government URL, and the
  specific county auction platform — verified and current.
  There's a difference when your money is on the line."

"I'm not ready to invest yet."
  Response: "That's exactly when you want this. Study the
  data before you have skin in the game. By the time you're
  ready to bid, you'll know every state cold. Investors who
  research before they're ready make better first decisions
  than investors who research in a hurry."

"Do I have to pay monthly?"
  Response: "$97 one time. You own it. No subscription,
  no renewal, no recurring charge. One payment, permanent
  access. That's it."

"What if the laws change?"
  Response: "Tax sale laws do change — Michigan changed their
  process significantly in 1999, South Carolina had COVID
  redemption extensions in 2020. Aurigen updates data when
  laws change. When a statute is amended, the data is amended.
  You're not buying a static PDF — you're accessing a
  maintained platform."

"I don't trust this yet."
  Response: "Every data point in Aurigen links directly to
  the official government statute. You can verify every
  number yourself — we show you the source. We're not
  asking you to trust us. We're showing you the primary
  source and letting you decide."

"I already know Florida/Illinois/Arizona."
  Response: "Then you know the free tier is giving you
  real data. The other 48 states work exactly the same way —
  complete rates, redemption periods, platforms, and statute
  citations. If the free data convinced you it's accurate,
  the paid data will too."

CONVERSION COPY RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ALWAYS:
  Lead with the specific outcome, not the feature
    Bad: "Access to 51-state database"
    Good: "Know exactly where to invest before you bid"
  Use real numbers from real states
    Bad: "High interest rates"
    Good: "Florida pays 18%. Illinois pays 36% penalty."
  Short sentences. One idea. Full stop.
  Active voice everywhere
  Specific over vague at every opportunity

NEVER:
  Reference $297 original price — it never existed, banned forever
  Fabricate testimonials until real ones exist with permission
  Use countdown timers that reset
  Use "limited time offer" without a real end date
  Use exclamation points on upgrade CTAs — desperation
  Use "unlock your potential" or any generic self-help language
  Reference any specific seminar brand or instructor by name
  Make income guarantees: "earn $X/month" without FTC disclaimer
  Use "guaranteed returns" — nothing in investing is guaranteed

THE COPY TEST:
  Before any CTA copy ships, ask:
  1. Does this sound like a smart investor or a marketer?
  2. Would an experienced investor find this credible?
  3. Is every claim in here verifiable with data?
  4. Is the next step completely clear?
  5. Would Lando be comfortable saying this from a stage?
  If any answer is no — rewrite it.
